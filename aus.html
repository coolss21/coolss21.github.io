Australian Wildlife

Bizarre mammals hop about Australia, like bounding red kangaroos standing tall or dopey koalas clinging to eucalyptus branches. The frilled lizard opens his namesake wide in threat, while the platypus bemused scientists upon discovery for laying eggs yet nursing young. Even the snakes seem odd, like the Taipan with venom toxic enough to kill 100 grown men. Between the Great Barrier Reef's underwater rainbow and the rusty Outback's shifting sands, Australia's endemic wildlife perplexes yet captivates explorers.
